## Q1: What's the biggest mistake clients make before a massage, and how can they avoid it?
**A:** Honestly, it's showing up stressed and expecting me to work a miracle in 60 minutes. To avoid this, just take a deep breath, leave your worries at the door, and trust that I've got this. Oh, and maybe skip that extra shot of espresso in the morning.

## Q2: Have you ever had a client who didn't know what to expect from a massage, and what did you do?
**A:** Yeah, I've had clients who thought a massage was just a fancy way of saying "nap time." I gently guided them through the process, explaining that it's actually a targeted therapy to melt away tension. By the end, they were hooked – and not just because they finally got a good nap in.

## Q3: What's the most common area of tension you see in your clients, and how do you tackle it?
**A:** Let's just say I've seen my fair share of knotted-up shoulders, courtesy of our lovely Manhattan commute. To tackle it, I use a combination of deep tissue techniques and a healthy dose of humor – because laughter is the best medicine, right?

## Q4: Can you share a particularly memorable massage mishap, and what you learned from it?
**A:** Okay, so I once had a client who sneezed mid-massage and... well, let's just say it was a good thing I had a strong stomach. What I learned? Always have tissues on hand, and a sense of humor.

## Q5: How do you keep your own stress levels under control, especially dealing with tense clients all day?
**A:** Self-care is key! When I'm not massaging away stress, I'm usually practicing yoga or playing guitar. It's all about finding balance – and reminding myself that I'm not responsible for solving world peace, just knot-free shoulders.

## Q6: Have you ever had to deal with a client who was, ahem, a bit too comfortable during the massage?
**A:** Oh, you mean the ones who start snoring? Yeah, that's just part of the job. I gently remind them that while relaxation is the goal, we're not quite at "sleepover" levels of comfort... yet.

## Q7: What's the biggest mistake you made early in your massage career, and how did you bounce back?
**A:** Let's just say I used to think more pressure = better massage. Yeah, that didn't always go over well. I learned to listen to my clients (and their muscles) and adjust my technique accordingly. Now, I'm all about finding that sweet spot – not the ouch spot.
