# What Clients Get Wrong: A Masseur's Rant 
=====================================================

As a professional massage therapist in Manhattan, I've seen it all - the good, the bad, and the "what were you thinking?!" In this post, we'll delve into the common misconceptions my clients have about massage therapy, and I'll do my best to set the record straight while keeping you entertained.

## The Myth of the "No Pain, No Gain" Approach
------------------------------------------

Some of my clients seem to think that the more pain they endure during a massage, the better the results will be. Newsflash: **that's just not true**! While some discomfort is normal, especially when working out knots, it shouldn't feel like I'm trying to torture you. If you're clenching your teeth and wondering why you didn't just stick with a root canal, something is wrong. Communication is key, folks! If it hurts, let me know, and I'll adjust the pressure.

## You Don't Have to be a Contortionist
--------------------------------------

I've lost count of how many clients come in thinking they need to be flexible enough to give Cirque du Soleil a run for their money. **Relax, folks**! Massage therapy is for every body, regardless of flexibility. Whether you're a yogi or a couch potato, my goal is to help you relax and ease any tension, not to turn you into a human pretzel.

## The Masseur is Not a Miracle Worker
--------------------------------------

While I'd love to claim that I can cure every ailment known to mankind with a single massage, **it's just not possible**. Chronic issues often require consistent therapy and, sometimes, a combination of treatments. Don't get me wrong; massage can work wonders, but it's not a replacement for medical care. If you have a serious condition, please consult with your doctor before scheduling a massage.

## Hygiene is Not Optional
-------------------------

This should go without saying, but **please, for the love of all things good and holy, shower before your massage**! As a masseur, I've encountered my fair share of, ahem, "fragrant" clients. Let's just say it's not exactly the most pleasant experience for either of us. A clean body is a happy body, and it makes my job a lot more enjoyable.

## You Don't Have to be Ashamed
------------------------------

Lastly, I want to address the elephant in the room: **body shame**. As a massage therapist, I've worked with clients of all shapes and sizes, and I can tell you that every body is unique and beautiful in its own way. Don't be afraid to get undressed and let me work my magic. I'm here to help you relax and feel good, not to judge you.
