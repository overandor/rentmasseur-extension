### Massage Myths Busted: Time to Get Your Rub On!
Are you one of those New Yorkers who thinks massage therapy is just for luxury-seekers or professional athletes? #### Myth #1: Massage is Only for Pampering
Many people think that getting a massage is an indulgent treat, something you do on a spa day or as a special occasion. But, let's get real, folks! Massage is not just about sipping champagne and getting cozy; it's about taking care of your body and mind. Regular massages can help alleviate chronic pain, improve sleep, and even boost your immune system. So, don't save massages for your birthday – make them a regular self-care ritual!

#### Myth #2: You Need to Be a Yogi or Athlete to Benefit from Massage
Oh, the misconception that massage is only for the flexible and fit! Listen, I've worked with clients of all shapes, sizes, and fitness levels, and I can tell you that everyone can benefit from a good massage. Whether you're a marathon runner or a marathon Netflix-watcher (no judgment!), massage can help you relax, reduce muscle tension, and even improve your range of motion. So, don't worry if you can't touch your toes – I won't make you do any downward-facing dog during our session, I promise!

#### Myth #3: Massage Hurts (and You'll Be Sore for Days)
Okay, I'll admit it: sometimes massage can be a little uncomfortable, especially if you're new to it or have some serious knots to work out. But, trust me, the temporary discomfort is worth it! A skilled massage therapist (ahem, like yours truly) will work with you to find the perfect pressure and technique to ease your tension and leave you feeling like a brand new person. And, yes, you might experience some soreness after a deep tissue massage, but that's just your body's way of saying, "Hey, thanks for finally taking care of me!"

#### Myth #4: You Have to Be Naked (or Nearly Naked) for a Massage
This one always makes me chuckle! While it's true that some massage techniques may require you to undress, you can always choose to keep your clothes on or wear comfortable, loose-fitting attire. I've worked with clients in everything from sports bras to swimsuits, and I can assure you that I'm a total pro at working around whatever you're wearing. So, don't let modesty hold you back from getting the massage you need – just come as you are (or not, we won't judge)!
