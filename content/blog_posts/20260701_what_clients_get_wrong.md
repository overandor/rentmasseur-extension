### The Massage Mishaps: What Clients Get Wrong (And How to Get it Right!)
As a seasoned Manhattan masseur, I've seen it all - the good, the bad, and the downright awkward. From clients who think a massage is a appropriate time to catch up on their favorite TV show ( spoiler alert: it's not) to those who believe a little "extra" pressure is just what they need (hint: it's usually not), there are plenty of misconceptions about what happens on my massage table. So, let's dive into the top massage mishaps and set the record straight.

#### The "I'll Just Check My Email Real Quick" Mistake
Newsflash: your inbox will still be there after your massage. In fact, it'll probably be more overwhelming than ever, with all the stress and tension you've accumulated from staring at screens all day. My massage room is a digital detox zone, folks! Leave your phone in your bag or, better yet, in the waiting area. Trust me, your mind (and your muscles) will thank you.

#### The "More Pressure is Better" Myth
I get it - sometimes it feels like a little extra pressure is just what you need to release those knots. But let me tell you, as a trained professional, I can assure you that more pressure is not always better. In fact, it can be downright painful (and not in a good way). A good massage is all about finding that sweet spot between relaxation and relief. So, don't be afraid to speak up if it's too much - or too little. Your body (and I) will appreciate it.

#### The "I'll Just Hold My Breath and Hope for the Best" Approach
Breathing is key, people! Not just in life, but also during a massage. Holding your breath will only tense up your muscles more, making it harder for me to do my job. So, take a deep breath in, and let it out slowly. Feel the tension melt away, and let your body surrender to the bliss of the massage.

#### The "I'll Just Book a Massage and Then Cancel at the Last Minute" Habit
Hey, I get it - life happens. But canceling at the last minute not only wastes my time, but also means someone else who really needs a massage might miss out. So, please, for the love of all things relaxing, be considerate of my schedule (and yours). Book your appointment, and then show up. Your body - and I - will thank you.

### So, What's the Right Way to Do It?
Now that we've covered what not to do, let's talk about how to get it right. A great massage experience is all about communication, relaxation, and a willingness to let go (literally). So, here are a few tips to get you started:

* Be open with me about what you need - whether it's a specific area of tension or a particular type of massage.
* Take deep breaths, and let your body relax into the experience.
* Don't be afraid to speak up if something doesn't feel right - or if it feels amazing (I love hearing that too!).
