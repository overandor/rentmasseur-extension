### The Unlikely Path to Bliss: Why I Became a Masseur
As a Manhattan masseur, I've had my fair share of curious clients asking, "What made you choose this line of work?" Well, let me tell you, it's a story of twists, turns, and a whole lot of knots – not just the kind I untangle on a daily basis.

#### From Tech to Touch
I used to be a software engineer, stuck in a cubicle, staring at screens all day. My social interactions were limited to commenting on code and debating the best coffee machine in the office. It was a far cry from the human connection I craved. One day, I found myself at a massage therapist's table, getting a much-needed massage after a particularly grueling project. As I lay there, feeling the tension melt away, a thought struck me: "This is what I want to do." Not just receive massages, but give them.

#### A Career 180
I know what you're thinking: "A software engineer turned masseur? Has the world gone mad?" But hear me out. I realized that my analytical mind, attention to detail, and knack for problem-solving could be applied to understanding the human body and its many quirks. Plus, I got to trade in my keyboard for a more... hands-on approach. It's been a wild ride ever since. I mean, where else can you wear sweatpants to work and call it a "uniform"?

#### The Training: Where Theory Meets Practice (Literally)
Massage school was an adventure in itself. Imagine a room full of adults learning how to give and receive massages – it's like a real-life game of "Operation" but with less buzzing and more ointment. We practiced on each other, which led to some interesting moments. Let's just say I've had my fair share of awkward encounters and accidental discoveries of hidden tickle spots.

#### The Payoff: Making Manhattanites Relax
Fast forward to today, and I couldn't be happier with my decision. There's no greater joy than seeing a client's face relax, their shoulders drop, and a sigh of relief escape their lips. It's a small victory, but it's mine. And let's not forget the stories I get to hear – from the stressed-out bankers to the overworked artists, each person's unique tale is a reminder of why I love this job.

#### So, Why Did I Become a Masseur?
In short, I became a masseur because I wanted a career that combined empathy, physicality, and the satisfaction of helping others unwind. And, let's be honest, it's a great way to get out of attending networking events where the only thing being "worked" is the open bar.
