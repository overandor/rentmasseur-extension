### The Massage Mishaps: What Clients Get Wrong (And How to Get it Just Right!)
As a seasoned Manhattan masseur, I've seen it all – from the "I-haven't-showered-in-weeks" clients to the "I-just-ate-a-whole-pizza-by-myself" ones (no judgment, though!). But seriously, there are some common misconceptions about massage therapy that I'm here to debunk. So, sit back, relax (pun intended), and let's get this massage party started!

#### The "I'm Too Sore" Syndrome
I get it, folks. You think that because you're feeling a bit creaky, you're not massage-ready. But trust me, that's exactly when you need it most! Massage is like a reboot for your muscles – it helps relieve tension, reduces inflammation, and gets you back to your awesome self in no time. Don't be like the guy who showed up to his massage appointment with an ice pack on his shoulder, saying, "I'm just here for the ambiance." (Yes, that happened.)

#### The "I'll Just Take a Nap" Expectation
Listen, I'm flattered that you think my massage skills are so relaxing, but this isn't a sleepover, buddy! While it's true that massage can be super calming, it's not meant to be a replacement for your bed. I've had clients start snoring mid-massage (true story), and let me tell you, it's a bit... awkward. So, please, for the love of all things massage, try to stay awake and enjoy the experience!

#### The "I'm Too Ticklish" Excuse
Okay, I get it. Some people are just more... sensitive than others (ahem). But massage isn't about tickling your funny bone (unless you're into that sorta thing, in which case, we can discuss); it's about working out those kinks and knots that have been plaguing you. And trust me, a good massage will have you laughing – with joy, not because you're being tickled like a feather!

#### The "I Don't Need to Breathe" Myth
You know, I've had clients who think that holding their breath during a massage is, somehow, going to make it more effective. Newsflash: it won't! In fact, deep breathing is an essential part of the massage experience. It helps your body relax, your muscles release, and your mind unwind. So, go ahead, take a deep breath in... and out... and let's get this massage magic happening!

### So, What's the Right Way to Do It?
Now that we've covered what not to do, let's talk about how to make the most of your massage experience. Here are some pro tips:

* Shower beforehand (your masseur will thank you)
* Communicate with your therapist (if it hurts, tell us! We're not mind readers)
* Breathe deeply and relax (you got this!)
* Don't be afraid to laugh or make funny faces (we've seen it all, and we won't judge)
