#### Pre-Massage Prep: Don't Be a Rookie
Before the massage even begins, there are a few things to consider. First, **communicate with your partner**. Discuss any areas of tension (not just the kind that requires a massage) and what you're hoping to get out of the experience. It's also wise to **coordinate your outfits**; you're both going to be in a state of undress, so wearing matching "I'm with stupid" t-shirts might not be the best idea.

#### The Massage: Relax, But Not Too Much
During the massage, **try to relax**. It's understandable to get a bit giggly or uncomfortable at first, but remember, this is a judgment-free zone (or at least it should be). Avoid **falling asleep** and drooling all over the massage table; it's just not cute. And please, for the love of all things good, **don't fart**. It's a couples massage, not a chemistry experiment.

#### Post-Massage Chit-Chat: The Art of Not Being Awkward
After the massage, you might feel like you're walking on clouds, but **don't forget to tip**. Your massage therapists have just spent an hour kneading out all your kinks; show them some love. When you're getting dressed and ready to leave, **keep the post-massage glow talk to a minimum**. No one needs to hear about how "connected" you and your partner felt during the massage. Save that for your couples therapist.
